# Executive Summary

The Ambience ExpenseFlow Sales Playbook provides a standardized sales methodology for acquiring, qualifying, demonstrating, closing, onboarding, and expanding customer relationships.

The goal is to deliver a consistent customer experience while maximizing customer value and recurring revenue.

---

# Sales Philosophy

We do not sell software.

We solve business problems.

Every sales conversation should focus on:

- Saving time
- Reducing costs
- Improving compliance
- Increasing visibility
- Simplifying audits
- Delivering measurable ROI

---

# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Target organizations typically:

- Have 25–5,000 employees
- Process employee reimbursements
- Have multiple approval levels
- Need expense reporting
- Require financial controls
- Are moving toward digital transformation
- Value AI and automation

---

# Primary Decision Makers

Typical buyers include:

- Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
- Controller
- Finance Director
- Accounting Manager
- IT Director
- Procurement Manager
- Operations Director

# Sales Process

## Stage 1 – Lead Generation

Sources:

- Website
- LinkedIn
- Referrals
- Conferences
- Webinars
- Marketplace listings
- Content marketing
- Partner referrals

Goal:

Generate qualified opportunities.

---

## Stage 2 – Qualification

Questions include:

- How are expenses managed today?
- How many employees submit expenses?
- What accounting system is used?
- How long do approvals take?
- What challenges exist?
- Are corporate cards used?
- What compliance requirements exist?

Outcome:

Qualified Opportunity.

---

## Stage 3 – Discovery Meeting

Objectives:

- Understand business goals.
- Identify pain points.
- Document current workflow.
- Understand stakeholders.
- Estimate ROI.

Deliverable:

Discovery Report.

---

## Stage 4 – Product Demonstration

Demonstrate:

- Expense submission
- Multi-line expense reports
- Receipt upload
- AI validation
- Manager dashboard
- Finance dashboard
- Audit center
- Reporting
- Query builder
- Executive dashboards

Always tailor the demonstration to the customer's workflow.

---

## Stage 5 – Solution Proposal

Proposal should include:

- Customer objectives
- Recommended edition
- Implementation plan
- Pricing
- Timeline
- Support plan
- Training plan

---

## Stage 6 – Proof of Concept (Optional)

For enterprise customers:

- Configure sample workflows
- Import sample data
- Demonstrate integrations
- Validate requirements

---

## Stage 7 – Negotiation

Topics include:

- Licensing
- Pricing
- Support
- Implementation
- Service Level Agreements (SLAs)
- Security requirements

---

## Stage 8 – Contract Execution

Complete:

- Subscription Agreement
- Order Form
- Security Review
- Legal Review

---

## Stage 9 – Customer Onboarding

Transition customer to Customer Success.

Deliver:

- Administrator training
- User training
- Go-live checklist
- Success plan

# Objection Handling

## "We already use another expense system."

Response:

Highlight ease of implementation, AI capabilities, modern user experience, and future roadmap.

---

## "Your competitors are larger."

Response:

Emphasize innovation, agility, customer partnership, and responsive product development.

---

## "Implementation sounds difficult."

Response:

Explain onboarding process, migration tools, training, and customer success support.

---

## "We don't need AI."

Response:

Position AI as an assistant that improves efficiency, accuracy, and compliance—not as a replacement for finance professionals.
