# 1. Executive Summary

This Data Retention Policy defines how Ambience ExpenseFlow stores, retains, archives, and securely disposes of information throughout its lifecycle.

The objective is to balance operational needs, legal obligations, regulatory expectations, security, and privacy.

---

# 2. Purpose

This policy helps organizations:

- Protect business records
- Meet legal obligations
- Reduce storage costs
- Improve security
- Support audits
- Protect customer privacy
- Maintain operational efficiency

---

# 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- Employees
- Managers
- Finance Teams
- Auditors
- Administrators
- Organization Owners

and all information processed by Ambience ExpenseFlow.

---

# 5. Retention Schedule

| Data Type | Recommended Retention |
|-----------|----------------------:|
| Expense Reports | 7 Years |
| Receipts | 7 Years |
| Audit Logs | 7 Years |
| User Profiles | Duration of Employment + Organizational Policy |
| Application Logs | 90 Days |
| Security Logs | 1 Year |
| Error Logs | 90 Days |
| Backup Archives | Organization Policy |

Actual retention periods should comply with organizational requirements and applicable laws.

---

# 6. Secure Storage

Data should be stored using secure cloud infrastructure.

Recommended protections include:

- Encryption at Rest
- Encryption in Transit
- Access Controls
- Role-Based Access Control
- Backup Verification
- Audit Logging

---

# 7. Archiving

Inactive information may be archived to reduce operational storage while maintaining availability for:

- Audits
- Financial Reporting
- Regulatory Requests
- Business Continuity

Archived data should remain encrypted and access-controlled.

---

# 8. Data Disposal

When retention periods expire and no legal hold exists, information should be securely deleted.

Recommended practices:

- Secure deletion
- Removal from active systems
- Removal from archived storage where appropriate
- Verification of deletion
- Documentation of disposal activities

---

# 9. Legal Holds

If litigation, investigations, or regulatory reviews require preservation of information:

- Scheduled deletion must stop
- Relevant records must be preserved
- Authorized personnel must approve release of the legal hold

---

# 10. User Requests

Where permitted by applicable law, users may request:

- Access to their information
- Correction of inaccurate information
- Export of eligible information
- Deletion of eligible information

Requests remain subject to:

- Organizational policy
- Financial record requirements
- Audit obligations
- Legal holds

# 12. Compliance

Organizations should align retention practices with applicable laws and standards, which may include:

- GDPR
- CCPA
- SOC 2
- ISO/IEC 27001
- Internal Financial Policies
- Government Record Requirements

Organizations are responsible for determining the specific retention periods applicable to their jurisdiction and industry.
